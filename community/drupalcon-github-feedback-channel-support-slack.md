# Drupal Slack — **GitHub** feedback, **DrupalCon** “qualifies?”, and **#support** (community thread)

**Context:** Informal **Drupal Slack** thread. **MP Singh** asked whether a **GitHub** project could be **showcased** at **DrupalCon** despite **not attending**, and wanted **feedback** (not praise) on early work—[**actools-pl/actoolsDrupal**](https://github.com/actools-pl/actoolsDrupal) (**installer** as **first part** of a vision where **Drupal** acts as a **controller** for a broader **academic ecosystem**).

## What went wrong at first

The first message was **unclear** (knee-jerk wording). **Jeremy Chinquist** could not tell whether the ask was **“showcase at DrupalCon without attending”** vs **“is this the right kind of project for DrupalCon later?”** vs **“please review my repo.”**

## Guidance (**Jeremy Chinquist**)

- **Channel:** Requests for **general feedback** on a **personal / lab** project belong in **`#support`** (or other **appropriate** channels), not wherever the first post landed—**“out of place here.”**
- **DrupalCon:**
  - **Sponsoring companies** often have a **stage** to **showcase** products/projects.
  - **Individuals** frequently run **BoFs** (**birds of a feather**) so **discussion** can happen on a **topic**—a plausible path for **deeper** conversation than a drive-by link drop.
- **Tone:** **Clarifying** what you need helps; **many** people are **willing** to give **feedback** and **help** once the ask is clear.

## OP follow-up

- **MP Singh** apologized for the **initial** phrasing, committed to **forum/channel rules**, and thanked **Jeremy**—self-described as a **physics teacher** who had **“got lost a little.”**

## Use

When someone **drops a GitHub link** in the **wrong** Slack channel; when explaining **where** to ask for **project feedback**; when **DrupalCon** pathways (**sponsor** showcase vs **BoF**) come up for **indie** or **academic** work.
