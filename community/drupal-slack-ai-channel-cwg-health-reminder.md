# Drupal Slack — **`#ai` thread escalation** and **Community Working Group** health reminder

**Context:** A thread in the **AI** Slack channel (framed by the poster as **AI and ethics**) started from a **third-party social video** (Facebook Reel linked in chat—not reproduced here). The discussion **spiraled**: broad claims about **major contributors**, **institutional buyers**, **“vibe coding”**, and **EU / Commission** use of **Drupal**; several participants could not see how the clip related to the project. The exchange included **personal insults**, **escalating tone**, and **off-topic** friction.

## Official community response (**AmyJune** — Community Working Group / **Community Health**)

Paraphrase of the moderator message:

- **Disagreement is allowed** on Drupal, contribution, **AI**, or project direction; **insults**, **personal attacks**, and **tone escalation** are not.
- If there is a **real concern** (contributor behavior, project direction, contracts), raise it **clearly** and **without attacking individuals**; otherwise **step back** so others can engage.
- **Invested parties:** take a **break** to regain perspective.
- **Seek first to understand, then to be understood** — see **[Drupal Values & Principles](https://www.drupal.org/about/values-and-principles#seek-first-to-understand-then-to-be-understood)**.
- **Community Health Team** can help with **conflict resolution** when needed.

## Use

**Reference** for **channel hygiene**: viral **off-platform** content rarely carries a **clear Drupal brief**; combine **topic discipline** (`#ai` vs other channels) with **[Values & Principles](https://www.drupal.org/about/values-and-principles)** when threads heat up. **CWG** / **Community Health** messaging above is the **actionable** takeaway.
